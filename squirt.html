Species: Sea Turtle
Home: Great Barrier Reef
Sea turtles are a migratory species and can be found all around the world in cold waters to warm beaches. Sea turtles mate at sea, then come ashore on beaches to lay their eggs. After laying their eggs in a hole in the sand the females go back to sea. After 60 days the baby sea turtles hatch and have to make the dangerous journey from the next to the water. Natural light at the horizon guides the hatchings to the ocean. Once in the water they go out to survive and grow to be adult sea turtles eventually.
